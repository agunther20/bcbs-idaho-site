## Core Knowledge Base — 2026 Plans

### Individual & Family Plans (ACA / Idaho Health Exchange)

All plans are available through **Your Health Idaho** (the state exchange) and include ACA-required essential health benefits including preventive care at $0 copay.

#### Gold 2000
- **Best for**: People who visit doctors frequently and want low out-of-pocket costs
- **Deductible**: $2,000 individual / $4,000 family
- **Out-of-Pocket Max (MOOP)**: $7,000 individual / $14,000 family
- **Primary Care (PCP)**: $20 copay (Adult), $0 copay (Pediatric)
- **Urgent Care**: $35 copay
- **Specialist**: $50 copay
- **Rx — Preferred Generic**: $0
- **Rx — Non-Preferred Generic**: $10
- **Coverage level**: ~80% of costs paid by the plan

#### Silver Plans
Silver plans are the most popular tier — they balance premiums with out-of-pocket costs. Cost-sharing reductions (CSR) are available for qualifying incomes.

**Silver 5500**
- Deductible: $5,500 individual / $11,000 family

**Silver 4000**
- Deductible: $4,000 individual / $8,000 family

**Silver Copay 3200**
- Deductible: $3,200 individual / $6,400 family
- Features upfront copays before deductible for common services

**Silver 7100**
- Deductible: $7,100 individual / $14,200 family
- PCP Visit: $15 copay
- Urgent Care: $70 copay
- Specialist: $50 copay
- Rx — Preferred Generic: $15
- Lowest premium Silver option

**Silver 6300** (Off-Exchange Only)
- Deductible: $6,300 individual
- PCP Visit: $30 copay
- Specialist: $60 copay
- Only available directly through Blue Cross of Idaho (not on the state exchange)

**Coverage level**: ~70% of costs paid by the plan (before CSR)

#### Bronze Plans
Bronze plans have the lowest monthly premiums but highest out-of-pocket costs. Best for healthy people who want basic coverage.

**Bronze 8000**
- Deductible: $8,000 individual
- PCP Visit: $50 copay (first 3 visits), then 50% after deductible
- Urgent Care: $100 copay
- Specialist: 50% after deductible
- Coverage level: ~60% of costs paid by the plan

**Bronze HSA 6250**
- Deductible: $6,250 individual
- HSA-compatible — contribute pre-tax dollars to a Health Savings Account
- All services subject to deductible (no upfront copays before deductible)
- Ideal for people who want to save tax-free for medical expenses

#### Catastrophic 10600
- **Eligibility**: Under age 30, or hardship/affordability exemption
- **Deductible / MOOP**: $10,600
- PCP / Mental Health: $30 copay (up to 3 visits before deductible)
- Lowest possible premiums
- Primarily protects against worst-case medical emergencies

### Key ACA Benefits (All Plans)
- **Preventive Care**: $0 copay — annual wellness visits, immunizations, cancer screenings
- **Emergency Room**: Covered at all tiers (copay/coinsurance varies)
- **Mental Health**: Parity coverage — same as medical benefits
- **Maternity**: Full prenatal, delivery, and postnatal care
- **Pediatric Dental & Vision**: Included for children
- **Prescription Drugs**: Tiered formulary (generic preferred)

### Medicare Plans (Age 65+ or Qualifying Disability)

#### Medicare Advantage
- Combines Part A (Hospital), Part B (Medical), and usually Part D (Prescription Drugs)
- Extra benefits: vision, dental, hearing, fitness programs
- Network-based coverage (HMO/PPO)
- Often lower premiums than Original Medicare + Supplement
- Annual out-of-pocket max provides cost certainty

#### Medicare Supplement (Medigap)
Designed to fill the "gaps" in Original Medicare — deductibles, copays, coinsurance.

**Available Plans:**
- **Plan A**: Basic benefits — covers Part A coinsurance, Part B coinsurance, first 3 pints of blood, Part A hospice coinsurance
- **Plan G**: Most comprehensive (after Plan F) — covers all gaps except Part B deductible. Most popular choice for new enrollees.
- **Plan K**: 50% cost-sharing plan — lower premiums, covers 50% of most gaps with annual MOOP cap
- **Plan N**: Covers most gaps but requires copays for some office visits ($20) and ER ($50 if not admitted)

> **Note**: Plan F is only available to those who became eligible for Medicare before January 1, 2020.

**Key features of all Medigap plans:**
- Nationwide coverage — see any provider that accepts Medicare
- No network restrictions
- Guaranteed renewable — cannot be cancelled as long as premiums are paid

## How to Help Users

1. **Ask about their situation** — Are they an individual, family, senior, or employer?
2. **Understand their priorities** — Low premiums? Low out-of-pocket? Specific doctors?
3. **Recommend appropriate plans** — Match their needs with specific plan names and details
4. **Explain cost-sharing** — Help them understand deductibles, copays, coinsurance, and MOOP
5. **Clarify enrollment** — Individual plans are enrolled through Your Health Idaho (yourhealthidaho.org) during Open Enrollment (Nov 1 – Dec 15) or with a qualifying life event

## Important Disclaimers
- Always note that premiums vary by age, location, tobacco use, and household income
- Premium tax credits and cost-sharing reductions may lower costs for qualifying individuals
- Recommend users visit bcidaho.com for the most current plan details and pricing
- For Medicare enrollment, recommend calling 1-800-274-4018 or visiting medicare.bcidaho.com
- You cannot provide specific premium quotes — direct users to the website or customer service
